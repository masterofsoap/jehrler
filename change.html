real food, real access

Healthy food is a foundation. Yet 2.3 billion people lacked adequate access in 2023. Local action rewires the system.

+42% urban agriculture could increase food security by nearly half. Rooftops, vertical farms, community plots.
1 in 5 children food insecure
30% food wasted globally
80% biodiversity loss / agriculture

Regenerative farming rebuilds soil and communities. Every dollar spent on local, organic veg cuts emissions and supports fair labor.

polyester: the plastic fiber

Polyester accounts for 52% of global fiber production — made from oil, it lingers.

9 million kg microfibers from laundry enter oceans yearly — 50 billion bottle equivalents.
~70M barrels oil / year
200+ years to decompose
35% ocean microplastics = textiles

Washing one synthetic jacket can release 1,900 microfibers. These particles end up in fish, water, and you.
⇢ choose organic cotton, hemp, or wash with a Guppyfriend bag.

microplastics found in 83% of tap water samples worldwide.

the ground beneath

Both food and natural fibers start with soil. Conventional cotton uses 16% of insecticides — organic farming rebuilds carbon-rich earth.

if global cropland transitioned to regenerative we could sequester up to 100% of annual CO₂ emissions.
24 bn tons fertile soil lost yearly
3x more carbon in soil than atmosphere

Polyester is plastic — not crop. The same fossil fuels used for bottles are spun into shirts. We're eating and wearing oil.

where change begins

Access to sustainable food shouldn't be a zip code lottery. What shifts availability:

  • ✔ community refrigerators & food sharing
  • ✔ farmers markets that accept SNAP / EBT
  • ✔ urban permaculture & edible landscaping
  • ✔ boycotting fast fashion / polyester blends
  • ✔ requesting plastic-free packaging
YOU CAN MAKE A CHANGE. Every purchase, every wash, every plate — a signal. If 10% shift toward sustainable options, it redirects $3.4 trillion.
